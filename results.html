iGEM IISER Berhampur

Studies show that Urinary Tract Infections (UTI) are the third most common bacterial infection in India. The main demographic affected by the same being the aging female population, with about 50-60% of women experiencing a UTI at least once in their lifetime. Population with type 2 diabetes is also under threat as a weakened immune system further complicates the scenario. Chronic UTI can lead to frequent recurrence, antibiotic resistance, and kidney infections. The prevalence of UTIs in rural India can be explained by the lack of good hygiene, sanitary practices, and awareness. The current diagnostic method requires collecting urine samples, culturing them in a laboratory setting and antibody based testing, which is a time consuming procedure and has limited availability at the point of care. It is while looking into the various roadblocks in the process of the same, that a group of students from IISER Berhampur was faced with the challenge of timely and precise detection of UTI at POC. To combat the same, we are aiming to develop a novel, cost-effective, sensitive, and point-of-care aptamer-based diagnostic kit that can detect uropathogens, Apt4UTI.
